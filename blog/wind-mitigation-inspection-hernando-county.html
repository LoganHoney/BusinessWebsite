What Is a Wind Mitigation Inspection?

A wind mitigation inspection analyzes specific design features and construction components that help your home resist damage from wind and hurricanes — and it’s critically important for insurance underwriting in Florida.

Roof Shape

Hip roofs generally perform better in wind events compared to gable roofs.

Roof Deck Attachment

How the roof deck is fastened to the home structure affects its wind resistance.

Roof Covering

Type and condition of roofing materials influence wind uplift performance.

Opening Protection

Presence of shutters or impact-resistant glass increases insurer confidence.

Why Insurance Companies Require It

In hurricane-prone states like Florida, insurers use wind mitigation inspections to assess how a structure will handle hurricane force winds. Homes with wind-resistant features are officially documented, which can translate into lower premiums because fewer claims are expected after a storm.

Unlike a full home inspection, a wind mitigation inspection verifies only the features that affect *wind resistance*. It is not a condition report on the entire home, but a focused evaluation of structural resilience — and insurers value this data when underwriting policies.

Typical areas evaluated include roof shape and materials, roof attachment methods, secondary water resistance, and opening protections such as shutters or impact-resistant glass.
