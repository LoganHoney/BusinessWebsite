What Is a 4 Point Insurance Inspection?

A 4 Point Inspection is a limited-scope evaluation requested by insurance companies to assess risk before issuing or renewing a homeowners policy.

Unlike a full home inspection, this inspection does not evaluate the entire property. Instead, it focuses strictly on the four systems that historically generate the highest insurance claims:

Roof

Age, condition, visible damage, remaining life expectancy, and signs of active leaks.

Electrical

Panel type, wiring material, grounding, and presence of outdated or recalled components.

Plumbing

Supply pipe materials, drain lines, leaks, and water heater age.

HVAC

System age, operational condition, and overall performance.

Why Insurance Companies Require It

Insurance carriers use 4 Point Inspections as an underwriting tool. Older homes present higher claim risk due to aging materials, outdated electrical panels, deteriorating roofing systems, and plumbing failures.


The inspection allows insurers to eavaluate insurability risk, determine policy eligibility, and request repairs before binding coverage.


In most cases, homes over 20 years old in Florida require a 4 Point Inspection before coverage can be approved.

Is There a “Pass” or “Fail”?

A 4 Point Inspection does not technically “pass” or “fail.”

Instead, the report provides documented condition details that allow the insurance carrier to decide whether repairs are required prior to issuing or renewing coverage.

If deficiencies are noted, insurers may request corrections before approving the policy.
